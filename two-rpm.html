Method: the only reliable way a mic separates forced cutting from chatter is to change the rpm. Tooth-passing and its harmonics are forced — they sit at z·rpm/60 and its multiples, so their frequency scales with rpm. Chatter rings the tool/spindle structure at its natural frequency, which does not move when you change rpm. Run the same depth/width at two rpm 15–25% apart (e.g. 3000 then 3600). A spectrum peak that shifts by the rpm ratio is forced; a peak that stays put is the chatter mode — that frequency is your fn at speed, and the rpm suggestions below put a whole number of vibration waves between teeth to dodge it. Each cut still uses the air gaps to cancel the coolant pump and shop noise.
