Aim a speaker at the tool. Sweep · tool in plays a tone that slides low → high → low while the mic records. Then pull the tool back and press Sweep · baseline. The app subtracts the two, so what is left is the tool answering — the peak of that difference is fₙ. Then Set M1 fₙ feeds it to the lobes.
Test method, unproven on a microphone: the speaker shakes the tool, the baseline subtraction cancels the speaker and the room. Prove it first on an easy ringer — a steel ruler clamped in a vise. If the difference peak tracks your hand tap and moves when you change tools, it works. If you only see fixed peaks that do not move, the mic cannot do it here and you keep the hammer tap above.

Workflow: 1 tap test fills M1/M2 → 2 lobes shows the pockets → run one → 3 record the cut in Tune, pick the real in-cut peak, Picked → M1 fₙ refines the map → 4 the library mines what you saved. Every WAV carries its full context (shared inputs + operation info + analysis snapshot) in a metadata chunk; the file stays a normal playable WAV. One mic owner at a time. Recordings cap at 3 minutes. Lobes engine: zero order frequency domain, multi mode, equal or differential pitch; out of envelope: low speed slotting (process damping), ae under 5 percent of Ø.
