What actually moves these numbers
Force & bending: D, z, ae, ap, fz, material, and stickout L. δ ∝ L³, so L dominates bending.
rpm: sets Vf, power and the regeneration phase only — it does not change static force or deflection.
Helix: smooths the force ripple over a rotation; negligible on mean force. Corner r: second-order edge/ploughing effect, not in this model.
Workpiece UTS/hardness/E inform the cutting coefficient but are not the input — kc1.1 and mc are.
What the Hayati paper adds
The same Altintas–Budak coefficients (Kt, Kr) that drive this force model also drive the analytical stability lobe diagram — one material table feeds both. The paper tunes the tool's natural frequency (hollow body + screwed core, H=Lh/Lf) to shift the lobes onto your rpm, turning "unstable" into "stabilizable" (+40–65% stable depth), and reframes the SLD into four zones: stable, unstable, stabilizable, destabilizable. Takeaway: lobes are movable, not fixed.
