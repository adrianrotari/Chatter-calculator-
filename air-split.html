Record a cut. The app cancels the coolant pump, spindle and shop noise using the quiet air gaps, masks tooth-passing, and judges chatter two ways: a surviving spectrum peak (red) and whether the cut repeats every spindle turn (polar). Click any peak on the spectrum to test it — you get the rpm to run to dodge it.
cut (raw) air baseline = pump+spindle+shop residual = real vibration tooth-passing (removed) your pick
Method (data only, no verdict): (1) subtract the coolant-on air gaps — removes coolant pump, shop noise, spindle idle. (2) Mask the full rotation comb, every rpm/60 harmonic: this cutter is variable pitch + variable helix, so its tooth-passing repeats once per revolution and its forced energy lands across the whole comb, not on one z·rpm/60 line. (3) rpm is auto-detected from the recording (the fold that repeats best), so the polar repetition % is trustworthy. What is left in red is the only place a chatter / tool-mode tone could be, since a mic can't read the tool natural frequency directly. Reading it: a chattering cut leaves one strong off-comb tone that repeats across cuts plus a high non-repeating %; a stable cut (Markus's, confirmed no chatter marks) leaves low broadband and ~13 to 21% non-repeating in the steady part of the cut. Click a spectrum peak to get the rpm that would put whole waves between teeth. Validate the chatter end against a known bad cut when one occurs.
