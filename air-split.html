Press Record live and start cutting. The app learns the coolant-on air level during repositioning, builds a baseline from it, and subtracts it from the cutting sound in real time. Tooth-passing is masked. What is left in red is the vibration the cut actually adds.
cut (raw) air baseline = pump+spindle+shop residual = real vibration tooth-passing (removed)
Method: cut spectrum minus air-gap spectrum cancels anything common to both — coolant pump, spindle harmonics, steady shop noise. The tooth-passing comb (z·rpm/60 and multiples) is masked because it is forced, not chatter. A peak that survives is real cutting vibration; a peak strong in the blue air baseline is the pump or machine, not the cut. Live mode learns the air level automatically from the quiet repositioning moments — keep the tool out of cut for a second or two now and then so it can refresh the baseline. A mic still cannot read the tool natural frequency.
