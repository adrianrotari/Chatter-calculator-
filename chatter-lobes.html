Enter the tap test natural frequency, stiffness and damping, the tool and the cut. The map shows the stable depth limit per spindle speed; the chips are the pocket speeds worth running.
Curve = stability boundary blim (axial, mm): below it the cut is stable, above it chatters. Engine reproduces the Schmitz machining database analytically (55 of 65 cases at 0.0 to 1.6 percent, incl. multi mode and variable pitch). Out of envelope: low speed slotting (process damping) and radial immersion under 5 percent of Ø. Pitch field accepts spacings (90,85,95,90) or positions (0,93,178,270); blank = equal. Kt is derived from kc1.1, mc and the mean chip thickness (Kt = kc1.1·hm−mc), so this page and the Chip·Force page share one coefficient set. Pick a material or enter custom; custom with mc=0 reproduces a constant Kt.
